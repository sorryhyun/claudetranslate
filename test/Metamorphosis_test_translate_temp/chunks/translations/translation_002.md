## Translation Notes

### Major Translation Decisions

1. **Voice transformation description**: Translated the voice change with clinical precision—"고통스럽고 통제할 수 없는 삐걱거리는 소리가 섞여 있었고" (painful and uncontrollable squeaking mixed in)—preserving the gradual realization that his voice has become inhuman. The echo effect ("메아리") and uncertainty create the communication barrier theme.

2. **Family dialogue hierarchy**: Maintained appropriate Korean speech levels:
   - Mother's voice: gentle and concerned ("어딘가 갈 거 아니었니?")
   - Father's voice: authoritative progression from gentle to warning ("무슨 일이야?" → "그레고르! 그레고르!")
   - Sister's voice: plaintive and worried ("몸이 안 좋은 거야?")
   - Gregor's responses: carefully enunciated, trying to sound normal

3. **Interior monologue**: Preserved Gregor's self-denial and rationalization. His dismissal of the voice change as "심각한 감기의 첫 번째 징후" (first sign of a serious cold) and "외판원의 직업병" (occupational hazard for travelling salesmen) shows his continued refusal to accept reality.

4. **Physical struggle precision**: Translated the detailed movement descriptions with clinical accuracy:
   - "자신을 조금만 부풀리면" (blow himself up a little) for the covers
   - "계속해서 다른 방향으로 움직이는 그 모든 작은 다리들" (all those little legs continuously moving in different directions)
   - "타는 듯한 통증" (burning pain) for the bedpost impact
   - Maintained the visceral, almost entomological observation style

5. **Sentence structure**: Preserved Kafka's long, complex sentences where natural in Korean. The passage describing the voice change (lines 5-10) maintains the continuous psychological flow through connecting particles rather than breaking into shorter sentences.

6. **Time pressure**: Used Korean time format "6시 45분" (quarter to seven, literally 6:45) and "7시" (seven o'clock) maintaining the mounting anxiety and temporal specificity.

7. **Spatial awareness**: Carefully translated the multiple doors and family members' positions—"머리맡 문" (door near his head), "옆문 중 하나" (one of the side doors), "반대편 문" (other side door)—creating the claustrophobic sense of being surrounded.

8. **The locked door motif**: Gregor's self-congratulation for his "조심스러운 습관" (cautious habit) of locking doors establishes the barrier between himself and society, a central Kafkaesque symbol. The Korean preserves the irony—what he considers prudent has now trapped him.

9. **Denial and rationalization pattern**: Maintained Gregor's characteristic psychological pattern:
   - Minimizing the transformation ("상상에 불과한 것", pure imagination)
   - Hoping it will resolve itself ("천천히 해소될지", slowly resolve themselves)
   - Attributing it to mundane causes (감기, cold)
   - Maintaining the absurdist tone where impossible crisis is treated practically

10. **Physical impossibility descriptions**: The passage "이것은 침대에서는 할 수 없는 일이다" (This is something that can't be done in bed) captures Gregor's practical reasoning about an impossible situation, central to Kafkaesque absurdism.

### Stylistic Considerations

- **Matter-of-fact tone**: Preserved the deadpan narrative voice describing extraordinary situation with mundane practicality
- **Repetitive struggle**: The repeated failures (legs moving uncontrollably, wrong direction, return to starting position) create psychological frustration—maintained through parallel sentence structures
- **Sensory details**: Maintained vivid physical sensations (squeaking sound, burning pain, fresh air, morning fog)
- **Interior vs. exterior**: Clear contrast between Gregor's internal rationalization and external family concern
- **Tragicomic elements**: The dark humor of Gregor complimenting himself on locking doors while unable to communicate his crisis

### Challenges Addressed

1. **Ambiguity preservation**: Maintained Kafka's deliberate vagueness about body parts—"몸의 아래 부분" (lower part of his body) without specifying exact anatomy. "작은 다리들" (little legs) keeps the plural without specifying number.

2. **Communication breakdown**: The voice transformation is central—Korean captures the painful squeaking ("삐걱거리는 소리"), echo effect ("메아리"), and listener uncertainty perfectly.

3. **Psychological complexity**: Gregor's simultaneous awareness and denial comes through in Korean. He describes his transformed voice clinically but dismisses it as a cold.

4. **Physical comedy**: The struggle with uncontrollable legs and failed attempts to exit bed has a darkly comic quality preserved through detailed description of futile efforts.

5. **Temporal anxiety**: Time markers (quarter to seven, seven o'clock) build pressure as Gregor realizes how late it is while physically unable to act.
