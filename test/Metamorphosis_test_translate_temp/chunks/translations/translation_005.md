## Translation Notes

### Major Translation Decisions

1. **Gregor's perspective shift**: Opening paragraph shows his increased calm ("훨씬 더 침착해졌다") contrasting with previous anxiety. He's now focused on others' reactions rather than his own transformation—psychological shift from denial to acceptance of changed state while still seeking social inclusion ("다시 사람들 사이로 끌어들여졌다고 느꼈다").

2. **Communication paradox**: "이제 다른 사람들은 그의 말을 이해할 수 없었지만, 그에게는 충분히 명확하게 들렸고" (they couldn't understand his words any more, although they seemed clear enough to him)—captures the tragic isolation. His perception vs. reality gap widens. Korean structure emphasizes the contrast through "~지만" (although/but).

3. **Auditory self-awareness**: "그는 더 이상 스스로 이것을 판단할 수 있는지 확신이 서지 않았다" (he was no longer sure he could judge this for himself)—Gregor losing confidence in his own perception. The Korean "더 이상...지 않다" (no longer) emphasizes the loss.

4. **Physical struggle sequence** (lines 18-41): The door-opening attempt required precise, clinical Korean to match Kafka's almost entomological description:
   - "다리 끝의 접착력을 이용해" (using the adhesive on the tips of his legs)—matter-of-fact insect capability
   - "제대로 된 이빨이 없는 것 같았다" (seemed to have no proper teeth)—realization moment
   - "매우 강한 턱으로 보완되었다" (made up for with a very strong jaw)—insect anatomy compensating
   - "갈색 액체가 나와 열쇠 위로 흘러 바닥에 떨어졌다" (brown fluid came from his mouth, flowed over the key and dripped onto the floor)—disturbing biological detail, deliberately vague, maintains clinical tone without being gratuitously gruesome
   - "어떤 종류의 손상을 일으키고 있음이 분명했다" (he must have been causing some kind of damage)—understated violence to self

5. **Chief clerk's observation**: "그가 열쇠를 돌리고 있어요" (he's turning the key)—simple statement but significant. Uses informal polite Korean (-요 ending) showing he's speaking to parents, not Gregor. This is first acknowledgment that Gregor is accomplishing something, though not meant as encouragement.

6. **Imagined encouragement**: "잘했어, 그레고르...계속해, 자물쇠를 계속 잡아!" (Well done, Gregor...keep at it, keep hold of the lock!)—what Gregor wishes they'd say. Uses informal, supportive Korean that family would use. The Korean "잘했어" (well done) is warm, encouraging—tragically absent in reality. The contrast between his fantasy and their actual silence/fear is heartbreaking.

7. **Self-encouragement**: "그래, 결국 자물쇠 수리공은 필요 없었어" (So, I didn't need the locksmith after all)—moment of triumph. Korean uses informal self-talk ("~었어"), showing Gregor's brief satisfaction at accomplishing task independently despite transformed body. Darkly ironic given what happens next.

8. **The revelation scene** (lines 43-59): Family's reactions to seeing Gregor required careful differentiation:

   **Chief clerk:**
   - "오!" (Oh!)—exclamation "그것은 바람 소리처럼 들렸다" (sounded like the soughing of the wind)—inhuman quality
   - "손을 활짝 벌린 입에 대고" (hand pressed against his open mouth)—gesture of horror
   - "마치 꾸준하고 보이지 않는 힘에 의해 밀려나는 것 같았다" (as if driven by a steady and invisible force)—slow, inexorable retreat

   **Mother:**
